Rocks & Minerals of Missouri

Pyrite (FeS2)

Pyrite is also known as fool's gold! The crystalline structure of pyrite can produce metallic crystals in the shape of cubes, octahedrons or pyritohedrons, and they commonly have striations on the crystal faces. It is found in many parts of Missouri and commonly occurs along with other minerals.

Galena(PbS)

Galena is the official state mineral of Missouri! It can form into cubic crystals. When it is freshly broken it has a bright gray to purple metallic luster. Galena is the major source of lead ore and Missouri is the nation's top produder of lead.

Calcite(CaCO3)

Calcite is commonly flourescent and can also be phosphorescent. It can appear opaque to transluscent and can be scratched easily. Limestone is composed of this mineral.

Quartz (SiO2)

Quartz is one of the most abundant minerals and is found in many colors and variations. It is harder than glass or steel. In northeastern Missouri, quartz geodes are common. These geodes may also contain calcite or other minerals.
